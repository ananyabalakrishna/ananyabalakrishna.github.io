Ananya Renuka Balakrishna

Postdoctoral Researcher

University of Minnesota


I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Aerospace Engineering and Mechanics Department at the University of Minnesota. I develop and apply physics-based models to investigate the links between material microstructures and material properties. I completed my PhD in Solid Mechanics and Materials Engineering at the University of Oxford as a Felix Scholar, before pursuing postdoctoral research at MIT as a Lindemann Postdoctoral fellow.


Recent News (Last update: Jan 26, 2019)
– Our paper "Order-disorder transition in nano-rutile TiO2 anodes: A high capacity low-volume change Li-ion battery material" is now published in Nanoscale
– Using our recently developed multiscale phase-field modeling method we demonstrated the diffusion-induced grain boundary migration phenomena in lithium battery electrodes. This modeling technique has a potential to be used as a design tool to crystallgraphically and microstructurally engineer battery materials with enhanced properties. Preprint

Ananya Renuka Balakrishna

Assistant Professor of Aerospace and Mechanical Engineering

(Starting Fall 2020 )

University of Southern California

  • Download CV


In this group, we will use a combination of theory, computation and experiments to investigate how microstructures evolve in materials, and how we can engineer microstructural patterns to enhance material properties. Members will develop physics-based models and pursue bench-top experiments to solve open problems in a wide range of materials used in batteries, memory devices and energy harvesters. Prospective graduate students with an interest in interdisciplinary theoretical and computational research are encouraged to write to me at (arenukab@umn.edu).
